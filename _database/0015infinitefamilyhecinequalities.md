## Infinite Family of HEC Inequalities

The holographic entropy cone (HEC) [[Holographic Entropy Cone]] is the set of all possible entanglement entropy vectors that can be realized by holographic theories. It is known that the HEC is a convex cone and can be described by a set of linear inequalities. 

### Lower Regions 

For the case of 5 regions, the HEC is fully characterized by a set of inequalities:

- [The Holographic Entropy Cone for Five Regions](/papers/2019-hernndezcuenca/) the characterization of the HEC for 5 regions.

For the case of 7 region, there is a new inquality found:

- [A Holographic Inequality for \\$N=7\\$ Regions](/papers/2023-czech/) the discovery of a new inequality for 7 regions.


### Inifinty Family of Inequalities 

In fact, there is an infinite family of inequalities that can be proven to hold for the HEC:

- [Holographic Entropy Inequalities and the Topology of Entanglement Wedge Nesting](/papers/2023-czech2/) the discovery of two infinite family of inequalities for the HEC.

- [Two Infinite Families of Facets of the Holographic Entropy Cone](/papers/2024-czech/) the proof that the two infinite family of inequalities are indeed facets of the HEC.
