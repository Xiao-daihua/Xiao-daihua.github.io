abstract: "Even though little is known about the quantum entropy cone for \\$N\\textbackslash geq4\\$ subsystems, holographic techniques allow one to get a handle on the subspace of entropy vectors corresponding to states with gravity duals. For static spacetimes and \\$N\\$ boundary subsystems, this space is a convex polyhedral cone known as the holographic entropy cone \\$\\textbackslash mathcal\\C\\\\_N\\$ for \\$N\\$ regions. While an explicit description of \\$\\textbackslash mathcal\\C\\\\_N\\$ was accomplished for all \\$N\\textbackslash leq4\\$ in the initial study, the information given about larger \\$N\\$ was only partial already for \\$\\textbackslash mathcal\\C\\\\_5\\$. This letter provides a complete construction of \\$\\textbackslash mathcal\\C\\\\_5\\$ by exhibiting graph models for every extreme ray orbit generating the cone defined by all proven holographic entropy inequalities for \\$N=5\\$. The question of whether there exist additional inequalities for \\$5\\$ parties is thus settled with a negative answer. The conjecture that \\$\\textbackslash mathcal\\C\\\\_5\\$ coincides with the analogous cone for dynamical spacetimes is supported by demonstrating that the information quantities defining its facets are primitive."
